How Transnet started

From 1899 to today, how did we grow?

The start of everything

In 1899, Alec Thornhill & Norwell Bury kicked off the revolution of Public Transport by creating the Thornhill & Bury Railway Co. (TBRC for short), they were the forefront
of the railway network as they unified the unoccupied lines of the network for passenger service. Alec and Norwell both we're proud of what they did as they brought a railway
network that was divided for years.

The evolution of the railway network really kicked off on September 19th, 1901 as the Benolla (now Fairmead) Line was the first electrified line that TBRC had created, the first electric
train, Z1, was the first to roll out of the production line and straight into passenger service. Day one was a success with everyone on the line, and the total passengers at
the end of the day was 8,924. It was revolutionary and never seen before in the network.

From that day forward, Thornsbury's railway network changed forever.

How Transnet came along

In 1998, network disbutions were arising, and the government had to step in with a crippling operator, TFTN (which was owned by MotionRail, a now defunct company). The government
had created a solution by creating Transnet, the transport operator for Thornsbury, as the remaining modes of public transport were not establisted and combined yet. TFTN completely was
removed in 2002 following an accident on the Southmead Line (ex-Tunbridge Wells) involving a test train that TFTN was planning on introducing.

From that day onwards, Transnet was appointed primary rail operator and transport company for Thornsbury under the provision of the Department of Public Transport

What's the future like for Transnet?

Here at Transnet, we're committed to bringing better transport, including improved train services, more bus routes and improved tram services. We are commited to meeting our Future Proof
plan before 2030. Some of our plans are currently in action and we are committed to getting all our plans completed before 2030.
